# seam v3 — carry-forward sketch (superseded by DESIGN-V3.md)

This is the carry-forward document written right after v2's six phases
finished and the first real data-science pass on the accumulated simulation
data was done. It captures where the next direction came from, not a final
design — everything below is now resolved and staged, see `DESIGN-V3.md`
for the actual design decisions and build plan (Phase 0-4), the same way
`V2.md` was superseded by `DESIGN-V2.md`. This file stays as the historical
record of the sketch that led there, the way `V2.md` and `LOG.md` are kept.

Read `DESIGN.md`, `DESIGN-V2.md`, and `LOG.md` first regardless — v3 doesn't
discard anything proven, it builds on it. The mechanics (scarcity produces
trade, the congestion fix, lead memory, the settlement/hatch mechanic,
narrative generation, the Postgres data layer) are settled and correct. What
changes in v3 is scale: one society becomes many.

## Why v3, in the user's own words

The prompt for this direction was a handful of scattered notes, written down
verbatim as they occurred, preserved here exactly rather than paraphrased:

> "seam -> self sustainable world, make seam into a watchable world?, its
> pretty fun man its hilarious, group the agents into societies with
> leaders"

That's the whole brief. Read plainly:

- **"self sustainable world"** — already true. The persistent service ticks
  on its own whether or not anyone's watching (proven in Phase 1). Not a new
  feature to build, just the thing to keep leaning into.
- **"make seam into a watchable world?"** — the real signal. Genuine,
  unprompted enjoyment watching the leads (particularly their deliberately
  silly names and real, grounded narrative) and watching a hard-seed
  settlement nearly collapse in real time. That reaction is data, not
  incidental.
- **"group the agents into societies with leaders"** — the concrete
  mechanic. Right now there's exactly one settlement with one roster and
  three leads. This asks for many.

## How this came up

This pivot came out of a broader conversation about seam's dual-use
potential - the observation that startups often start with a narrow, benign
pitch and drift toward something more extreme (the example given: AI-powered
drone package tracking, with the real intent being military use), and
whether the same lens applied honestly to seam. Two other pivots came out of
that same conversation and live in their own separate repos, deliberately
not folded into seam itself:

- `seam-logistics` - the same mechanic retargeted at real supply-chain and
  resource-allocation planning. Straightforwardly commercial, no ethical
  shadow.
- `seam-swarm` - a conceptual exploration of the same lead/standing-order
  structure re-themed as swarm/unit command-and-control - the closest
  structural match to the drone example. Explicitly simulation-and-data-only,
  with a written boundary against ever moving toward real-world actuation.

This one - Watchable World - was the one actually recommended out of that
conversation, specifically because it's what the user's own prior notes and
genuine reaction were already pointing at, not because it was the safest
option by default.

## The shared mechanic all three pivots actually need

Generalizing from one settlement to *N* independent societies, each with its
own roster and its own leadership, is the single piece of work that unlocks
all three pivots at once - not just this one:

- Here, societies become rival or cooperating factions - real stakes, real
  reasons to keep watching, narrative that reads across societies instead
  of one settlement.
- In `seam-logistics`, societies become distribution regions that can trade
  with or compete against each other for shared resources.
- In `seam-swarm`, societies become "sides" contesting territory instead of
  one settlement being stewarded or neglected.

## Rough shape, not yet a real plan

Genuinely open, to be resolved when `DESIGN-V3.md` gets written for real:

- How many societies, and does that number stay fixed or can new ones form
  / old ones fail entirely (a society hitting zero population - does it
  just end, or does something more interesting happen)?
- Do societies compete over the *same* shared world (one graph, multiple
  settlements on it, real contention for the same resource nodes), or do
  they exist on separate/adjacent worlds that only interact through trade
  and narrative, not physical proximity? This changes a lot about what
  "rivalry" mechanically means.
- Does each society need its own hatch (multiple player-controllable
  characters), or does the player possess whichever one they choose at a
  time?
- What does the viewer look like with N societies instead of one - still
  one page, or does it need real navigation between them?
- Replay/highlight extraction - genuinely new work, not a port of anything
  from v1/v2. What actually counts as a "highlight" worth surfacing (a
  death, a rivalry escalating, a lead's memory summary turning notably
  more dramatic)?

## What's carried over unchanged

Everything from v2: the Rust core, the persistent service architecture, the
lead memory system (mechanical caution + LLM self-summary), the standing
orders fix (leads can now actually choose to issue one, verified in live
unattended play), the narrative-generation approach (grounded status
reports, not open-ended scene-writing, after the hallucination caught in
Phase 4), and the Postgres data layer. v3 is a scale change to an already-
proven foundation, not a rebuild.
