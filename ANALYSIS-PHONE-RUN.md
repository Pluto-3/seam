# Analysis: the phone-access overnight run (2026-07-20/21)

Real critical analysis of the world launched for phone access over WiFi (`serve --agents 40 --nodes 15 --societies 3 --seed 42`, `sidecar.py` against real Ollama), left running ~9.5 hours unattended and shut down clean. Preceded by an outside-research pass on analysis methodology (see `LOG.md`, 2026-07-21) — this document reports what that research pointed at, applied to this run's actual data.

**Data available, and what isn't.** This run wasn't started with `--log-path` or `--postgres-url`, so there is no raw per-tick event log for it — only `core-rs/logs/run_20260720_175321_phone_{stats,societies}.csv` (periodic global/per-society snapshots, ~150 real ticks apart, 1148/3444 rows) and the sidecar's own decision log (`..._sidecar.jsonl`, 38,301 real entries: 34,461 lead decisions, 3,456 memory summaries, 384 narrative scenes). Every analysis below is scoped to what that actually supports — several of this project's existing `analyze_*.py` scripts need a raw JSONL log this run doesn't have, so techniques were adapted rather than the scripts reused verbatim (new scripts: `analyze_changepoints_stats.py`, `analyze_trade_network_sidecar.py`, `analyze_goal_drift.py`, `analyze_cross_society_stats.py`; `analyze_llm_narratives.py` reused as-is, its schema matched exactly).

## 1. Changepoint detection: a genuinely flat equilibrium, verified not assumed

Ran `ruptures`' PELT (the project's own established penalty convention, `3 * log(n) * var`) on `specialization_index`, `trades_window`, and `avg_hunger` across all 1148 real rows. **Zero real changepoints detected in any of the three** — the whole 171,000-tick run is one statistically flat segment. Checked this wasn't a too-strict-threshold artifact by sweeping the penalty down two orders of magnitude: it doesn't reveal one hidden large shift, it just fragments into dozens of noise-level segments (104+ "changepoints" at the loosest setting tried, clearly overfitting). Honest conclusion: **this economy reached equilibrium before the very first 30-second snapshot and never left it for 9.5 hours** — the most stable run this project has produced.

## 2. Trade network: lead-centric, real, well-distributed

19,979 of 34,461 lead decisions (58%) were TRADE — the dominant action for all three leads. Scope limit stated plainly: this is a **lead-initiated** network only (crowd agents aren't LLM-driven and never appear in this log), and it reflects what a lead *chose*, not confirmed successful resolution (no raw event log to confirm outcomes against). Within that scope:

- 92.9% of trade attempts targeted crowd agents, only 7.1% targeted other leads/hatches — consistent with everything observed live this session, now confirmed at 20,000-decision scale instead of a handful.
- All 46 population members were touched by all 3 leads' trade attempts at some point over 9.5 hours — **not evidence of shared favorites** (checked properly): trade is fairly well-distributed per lead, top-5 partners account for only 23-26% of each lead's own trade attempts across ~45 distinct partners, with a real but modest skew (max partner ~3-4x the median).
- Net resource flow into lead-initiators: ore +2342, wood +335, food **−2677**. Leads are net food-exporters and net ore-importers — they're using their own food surplus as trade currency to acquire ore, a real, coherent economic pattern this data hadn't shown before.

## 3. Goal drift: none found, checked directly and rigorously

Applying the GD_actions framing from the 2026 LLM-agent-drift literature: split each lead's decisions into early/middle/late thirds (by real timestamp) and compared action-type distributions and explicit goal-resource-seeking rates. **Action mix and goal-adherence stayed flat within 1-3 percentage points across the entire run for all three leads** — e.g. lead1 and lead2's share of TRADE attempts specifically seeking wood moved by +0.2% and +0.7% respectively from early to late, both indistinguishable from noise. A real, clean null result: **no measurable long-horizon goal drift in this run**, worth taking at face value rather than searching harder for a story.

One nuance caught and explained rather than left as a false "inconsistency": lead2 (wood specialist, goal to out-produce at its specialty) shows a *low* wood-trading share (~18%). That's not goal failure — a wood specialist gathers wood efficiently on its own and trades its surplus *away* for the ore/food it isn't specialized in, so low wood-*trading* is exactly what correct specialty-driven behavior looks like. (Also worth noting as a real structural fact, not a bug: lead1's specialty is Food by the code's fixed `RAW_RESOURCES` cycling, but its stated goal is about *wood* — goal text and specialty assignment are independent in this project's design, and this is the one lead where they don't line up.)

## 4. Cross-society comparison: zero deaths everywhere, weak-but-real shared dynamics

All three societies held their exact starting roster (8/8) for the entire 9.5-hour run — **independently confirmed zero deaths in each society**, not just in the global aggregate. Population correlation between societies is undefined (constant input, correctly flagged rather than a silent NaN) since nothing ever changed. For the metrics that did move — energy, hunger, food — correlations between society pairs are real but modest (r = 0.05 to 0.22, mostly statistically significant given the ~1148-row sample size, strongest for `total_food_held` at r=0.15-0.22). Honest read: the three societies are **not fully independent** (they share one world, plausibly some shared resource-cycle effects), but each still has a visibly distinct trajectory (different means, different specific fluctuation timing) — not one society's story dominating the other two, and not three coincidental reruns of the same thing either.

## 5. Self-perception vs. reality: `analyze_llm_narratives.py`, reused as-is

100% of 34,845 decision prompts got a real LLM answer across the full 9.5 hours — Ollama never once fell back to mechanical autopilot, a first for this project's long-running instances. Mean memory-summary sentiment: +0.399 (VADER, leans positive). Sentiment tracks real trade performance only weakly (`rho=+0.138, p=3.5e-16` against `trade_success_ratio`) — statistically real given the sample size, but a loose coupling, not a tight one. One methodology catch worth remembering for any future pass: the "most undersold" examples (negative VADER score, strong real performance) read as clearly triumphant in plain English ("outmaneuvering Hatch0 189 times") — VADER is likely mis-scoring competitive-metaphor language ("battles," "breathing down my neck") as negative, not picking up on a real emotional undertone. Treat that specific sub-finding as a tool limitation, not a real signal.

## Overall take

The headline finding across all five angles is the same shape: **this was an unusually stable, unusually consistent run** — no regime shifts, no deaths anywhere, no measurable goal drift, and a trade network that's broad rather than cliquish. That's a real, useful data point about what this design looks like at genuine scale (9.5 hours, ~171K ticks, ~35K real LLM decisions) rather than the shorter stress-tested runs this project has mostly analyzed before now. It's also, honestly, a *less dramatic* dataset than the deliberately-hard-seeded collapse runs from the July 17-19 pass (`ANALYSIS.md`) — this seed (42) simply didn't produce a crisis to analyze the way seed 19/31 did. Worth remembering before assuming "stable" is the design's general property rather than this particular seed's.
